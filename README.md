In high-density interaction and strong occlusion scenarios, multi-object tracking often fails due to scale instability, depth ordering confusion, and identity swapping. To address this issue, we propose Multi-Level Spatial Consistency (MSC-Track), which enhances geometric constraints and association robustness through multi-level spatial consistency. The method consists of three complementary mechanisms: Local Scale Stability models temporal scale consistency and adaptively adjusts according to uncertainty; Vertical Distance Cue uses the bottom edge of the target to construct an uncalibrated depth order, providing more reliable spatial cues in overlapping areas; Global Perspective Map learns perspective mapping online, unifying the heights of targets in different vertical bands to a geometrically equivalent scale. The three cues work collaboratively in the sorting-threshold decoupling framework, effectively reducing matching ambiguity and identity drift caused by occlusion. On the highly challenging DanceTrack-test, MSC-Track achieves significant performance improvement. HOTA increases by 1.3, DetA increases by 1.5, IDF1 increases by 0.7, and MOTA increases by 2.3 simultaneously, fully demonstrating the robustness of the method under unified appearance and complex nonlinear motion conditions. Visual results further show that MSC-Track outperforms existing methods in identity recovery after long-term occlusion and the ability to maintain ID in target intersections, proving its effectiveness and practical application value in complex multi-object tracking scenarios.
